correlation_user = df['Global_Sales'].corr(df['User_Score'])

print(f"Correlation between Global Sales and User Score: {correlation_user:.2f}")

import numpy as np

# Define bins and labels for Score_Category
bins = [0, 59, 74, 89, 100]
labels = ['0-59 (Poor)', '60-74 (Average)', '75-89 (Good)', '90-100 (Excellent)']

# Create the Score_Category column
df['Score_Category'] = pd.cut(df['Critic_Score'], bins=bins, labels=labels, right=True, include_lowest=True)

# Log-transform Global_Sales for better interpretability
df['Log10_Global_Sales'] = np.log10(df['Global_Sales'] + 1)

# Create the box plot
plt.figure(figsize=(12, 7))
sns.boxplot(x='Score_Category', y='Log10_Global_Sales', data=df, order=labels, showfliers=False)
plt.title('Global Sales by Critic Score Category')
plt.xlabel('Critic Score Category')
plt.ylabel('Log10(Global Sales + 1)')
plt.grid(axis='y', linestyle='--', alpha=0.7)
plt.show()

My question can somewhat be answered by the available data. The trendlines do have an upward slope, so that's a good start. There is weak correlation with sales and critic score, but for user score it doesn't even qualify as weak. The box plot, though, sure makes it look like the sales get very clearly higher with better review categories. An insight I discovered was that the better reviewed games each have a get more and more spread out based on the box and whisker plot. This wouldn't be due to outliers, as even the quartiles clearly get more spread out as we move rightward.


An unexpected finding was how weak the correlation was for reviews and sales. I expected it to be strong, with the notion that better reviewed games get more hype and, thus, more people are interested in them. I wonder if my analysis is limited by the games that have very few reviews. Metacritic uses the mean of reviews for its rating, and if only three people have given a game a review then it could be heavily skewed. For further investigation, I would trim this bulky dataset even more than I already did. I cleaned out games with null data, but I think if we just trimmed that to the top 1,000 games we might have better data. Or if we could figure out a way to get rid of games that don't have enough reviews to get a good grasp of its quality. Also, the scatterplots are terrible and useless with all that data. It's just a big blob at the bottom.
